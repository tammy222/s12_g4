Fry-up (English Breakfast)

Ingredients:

For the fry-up:

  • 2 sausages
  • 2 slices of bacon
  • 1-2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup baked beans
  • 1 tomato, halved
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 slices of bread (for toast)
  • 1 tablespoon butter (for frying and spreading)
  • Black pudding (optional)
  • Hash browns (optional)

Instructions:

1. Cook the sausages and bacon:
In a large frying pan over medium heat, cook the sausages until browned and cooked through, about 8-10 minutes. Add the bacon to the pan and fry until crispy, turning occasionally. Set aside and keep warm.
2. Fry the mushrooms and tomatoes:
In the same pan, add a little butter if needed and sauté the sliced mushrooms until golden brown. Place the tomato halves cut side down in the pan and cook until softened and slightly charred. Set aside with the sausages and bacon.
3. Heat the baked beans:
In a small saucepan, gently heat the baked beans over low heat, stirring occasionally until warmed through.
4. Fry the eggs:
In the same frying pan, fry the eggs to your preference (sunny side up, over easy, etc.). You can also scramble the eggs if preferred.
5. Toast the bread:
Toast the slices of bread until golden, then spread with butter.
6. Assemble and serve:
On a large plate, arrange the sausages, bacon, fried eggs, baked beans, mushrooms, tomatoes, and toast. If using black pudding or hash browns, add them to the plate. Serve hot with a cup of tea or coffee. Enjoy your hearty English breakfast!

Italy

Spaghetti

Ingredients:

For the spaghetti:

  • 400g (14 oz) dried spaghetti
  • Salt (for boiling water)

For the sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Cook the spaghetti:
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
2. Prepare the sauce:
While the spaghetti is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for another minute.
Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes (if using). Season with salt and pepper. Simmer the sauce for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and the flavors meld together.
3. Combine the spaghetti and sauce:
Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce and toss to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a small amount of the pasta cooking water to loosen it up.
4. Serve:
Serve the spaghetti hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese. Enjoy your delicious spaghetti!

Egypt

Shakshouka

Ingredients:

For the shakshouka:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4-6 large eggs
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • Crusty bread (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the sauce:
In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and red bell pepper and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using), and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
Add the diced tomatoes and tomato paste, stirring to combine. Season with salt and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for about 10 minutes, until it thickens and the flavors blend together.
2. Add the eggs:
Make small wells in the sauce and gently crack an egg into each well. Cover the skillet and cook on low heat for 5-8 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks remain runny. You can cook longer if you prefer firmer yolks.
3. Garnish and serve:
Remove from heat and garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley and crumbled feta cheese (if using). Serve hot with crusty bread for dipping into the sauce. Enjoy your flavorful shakshouka!

Ethiopia

Beyaynetu (Ethiopian Vegetarian Platter)

Ingredients:

For the injera (Ethiopian flatbread):

  • 2 cups teff flour (or a mixture of teff and all-purpose flour)
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil (for greasing the pan)

For the toppings (variety of vegetarian stews):

For Misir Wot (Spicy Red Lentil Stew):

  • 1 cup red lentils
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon berbere spice mix
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • Salt to taste

For Kik Alicha (Mild Split Pea Stew):

  • 1 cup yellow split peas
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

For Gomen (Collard Greens):

  • 1 bunch collard greens, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. Prepare the injera:
In a large bowl, mix the teff flour and water until smooth. Cover and let the batter ferment at room temperature for 2-3 days, until it becomes bubbly and sour. When ready, stir in the salt.
Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and lightly grease with oil. Pour a thin layer of the batter into the pan, swirling to coat evenly. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges begin to lift. Do not flip the injera—remove from the pan when the top is set. Repeat with the remaining batter.
2. Prepare the Misir Wot:
In a pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened. Stir in the garlic and berbere spice mix, and cook for another minute. Add the lentils, diced tomatoes, and water to cover. Simmer for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lentils are tender and the stew thickens. Season with salt.
3. Prepare the Kik Alicha:
In another pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and cook until softened. Stir in the turmeric, split peas, and enough water to cover. Simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the split peas are soft. Season with salt.
4. Prepare the Gomen:
In a large pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger, and sauté until softened. Add the chopped collard greens and a splash of water. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the greens are tender. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Serve:
Arrange the injera on a large platter and spoon the stews—Misir Wot, Kik Alicha, and Gomen—onto the injera. Serve with additional injera on the side. Enjoy your delicious Beyaynetu, a traditional Ethiopian vegetarian feast!

South Africa

Bunny Chow

Ingredients:

For the curry:

  • 500g (1 lb) beef, chicken, or lamb, cubed (or use vegetables for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup water or stock
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)

For the bread:

  • 1 loaf of white bread (unsliced, large enough to cut into quarters)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the curry:
In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until golden brown. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute.
Stir in the curry powder, cumin, coriander, and turmeric, cooking for 1-2 minutes to release the flavors. Add the meat (or vegetables) and cook until browned on all sides.
Add the diced tomatoes, water (or stock), and cubed potatoes. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Prepare the bread:
Cut the loaf of bread into quarters. Hollow out the inside of each quarter to create a bowl, leaving the crust intact. Keep the removed bread aside for dipping.
3. Serve:
Spoon the curry into the hollowed-out bread bowls. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with the bread pieces you removed earlier for dipping. Enjoy your flavorful South African Bunny Chow!
