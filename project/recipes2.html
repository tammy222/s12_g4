Pho

Ingredients:

For the broth:

  • 1.5kg (3 lbs) beef bones (marrow, knuckle, or oxtail)
  • 500g (1 lb) beef brisket
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 ginger root, halved
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 star anise
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons rock sugar (or regular sugar)
  • 3 liters water (or enough to cover bones)

For the soup:

  • 400g (14 oz) pho rice noodles
  • 300g (10 oz) beef (sirloin or eye round), thinly sliced
  • Bean sprouts
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, basil, mint)
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Thinly sliced chili peppers (optional)
  • Hoisin sauce (for serving)
  • Sriracha (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the broth:
Rinse the beef bones and brisket under cold water. In a large pot, cover the bones with water, bring to a boil, and simmer for 10 minutes. Drain and rinse the bones to remove impurities.
In the same pot, add the cleaned bones, brisket, onion, ginger, cinnamon, star anise, cloves, coriander seeds, and enough water to cover everything. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 3-4 hours. Skim off any foam that forms on the surface.
After 3-4 hours, remove the brisket and set it aside to cool. Strain the broth through a fine mesh sieve and discard the solids. Season the broth with fish sauce, sugar, and salt to taste.
2. Prepare the noodles:
Cook the pho noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
3. Assemble the pho:
Thinly slice the cooked brisket. Divide the cooked noodles into serving bowls. Add a few slices of brisket and raw beef on top.
4. Serve:
Pour the hot broth over the beef and noodles. The hot broth will cook the raw beef slices. Serve with bean sprouts, fresh herbs, lime wedges, hoisin sauce, and sriracha on the side for added flavor. Enjoy your delicious bowl of Pho!

Thailand

Tom Yum

Ingredients:

For the soup base:

  • 4 cups water or chicken stock
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves, torn into pieces
  • 1-2 Thai bird’s eye chilies (adjust to taste), chopped
  • 3 slices galangal (or ginger as a substitute)
  • 200g (7 oz) shrimp or chicken (sliced thinly)
  • 1 cup mushrooms (straw mushrooms or any variety)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1-2 tablespoons lime juice (adjust to taste)
  • 1-2 teaspoons Thai chili paste (nam prik pao) (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Optional Add-ins:

  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Onion, sliced
  • Bamboo shoots
  • Fresh Thai basil (for garnish)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the broth:
In a medium pot, bring the water or chicken stock to a boil. Add the lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes to infuse the flavors.
2. Add the proteins and vegetables:
Add the shrimp (or chicken) and mushrooms to the broth. If you're using tomatoes or onions, add them now. Simmer until the shrimp turns pink and is cooked through, about 3-4 minutes (or until the chicken is fully cooked).
3. Season the soup:
Add the fish sauce, lime juice, and chopped chilies. Taste the soup and adjust seasoning—more fish sauce for saltiness, more lime juice for sourness, or more chilies for spice. If you're using chili paste, stir it in now for extra heat and depth of flavor.
4. Serve:
Remove from heat and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with a side of jasmine rice, or enjoy it as a stand-alone dish. Enjoy your flavorful Tom Yum soup!

India

Curry

Ingredients:

For the curry base:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup water or chicken broth
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the protein and vegetables:

  • 500g (1 lb) chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu (cubed)
  • 2 medium potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup carrots, chopped
  • 1 cup peas (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the curry base:
Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until soft and golden, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute.
2. Add the spices:
Stir in the curry powder, cumin, coriander, and turmeric. Cook the spices for 1-2 minutes to release their aroma, stirring constantly to avoid burning.
3. Add the liquid:
Pour in the coconut milk and water (or chicken broth). Stir well to combine the spices with the liquid. Add the chopped tomatoes and bring the mixture to a simmer.
4. Add the protein and vegetables:
Add your choice of protein (chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu) to the curry sauce, followed by the potatoes, carrots, and peas. Simmer until the protein is cooked through and the vegetables are tender, about 20-25 minutes.
5. Season and serve:
Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the curry hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and lime wedges on the side. Enjoy your delicious curry with rice or naan!

Meat Pie

Ingredients:

For the pastry:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1/4 to 1/2 cup ice water
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

For the filling:

  • 500g (1 lb) ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup beef or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon thyme (optional)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the pastry:
In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cubed butter and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
Gradually add ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough begins to come together. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
2. Prepare the filling:
In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and carrot, and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
Add the ground beef (or lamb) and cook until browned. Stir in the tomato paste, peas, broth, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the filling to thicken. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool.
3. Assemble the pie:
Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F). Roll out half of the chilled pastry dough on a floured surface and line the bottom of a pie dish. Pour the cooled filling into the pastry shell.
Roll out the remaining dough and cover the top of the pie. Crimp the edges to seal, and cut a few small slits on top to allow steam to escape. Brush the top with beaten egg for a golden finish.
4. Bake:
Bake the meat pie in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Let the pie cool slightly before slicing and serving.
5. Serve:
Serve warm with your favorite sides like mashed potatoes or a fresh salad. Enjoy your hearty homemade meat pie!

Khachapuri

Ingredients:

For the dough:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the cheese filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 cup ricotta or farmer’s cheese
  • 1 egg (for egg wash and for serving on top)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the dough:
In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit for about 10 minutes until frothy.
In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour, mixing until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes, until smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
2. Prepare the filling:
In a bowl, mix the mozzarella, feta, and ricotta (or farmer’s cheese) until well combined. Set aside.
3. Shape and fill the khachapuri:
Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into 2-3 equal portions. Roll each portion into a circle or oval shape, leaving the edges slightly thicker. Spoon the cheese mixture into the center of the dough, spreading it evenly but leaving a border around the edges.
Fold the edges of the dough over the cheese filling slightly to form a boat shape, pinching the ends to seal.
4. Bake and serve:
Bake the khachapuri in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly. Remove from the oven and gently crack an egg in the center of the cheese filling. Return to the oven for 3-5 minutes, just until the egg white sets but the yolk remains runny.
Remove from the oven, add a small pat of butter on top of the hot cheese, and serve immediately. Enjoy your delicious Khachapuri warm!
