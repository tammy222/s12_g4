Tacos

Ingredients:

For the taco filling:

  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef (or chicken or turkey)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup water or beef broth

For the taco assembly:

  • 8 small taco shells (corn or flour tortillas)
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Chopped tomatoes
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or your choice)
  • Sour cream
  • Guacamole or sliced avocado
  • Salsa or hot sauce (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro (optional)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Cook the taco filling:
In a large skillet, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened, about 2-3 minutes. Add the ground beef, breaking it up with a spatula, and cook until browned and fully cooked, about 6-8 minutes.
Once the meat is cooked, drain any excess fat. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add 1/4 cup of water or beef broth and simmer for another 2-3 minutes, until the mixture is thickened and flavorful.
2. Warm the taco shells:
Warm the taco shells in a dry skillet or wrap them in foil and heat them in the oven for a few minutes, or microwave them for about 20 seconds.
3. Assemble the tacos:
Place a spoonful of the seasoned taco filling into each shell. Top with shredded lettuce, chopped tomatoes, shredded cheese, sour cream, guacamole or avocado, salsa, and any other toppings you like.
4. Serve:
Garnish the tacos with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges for a zesty finish. Enjoy your delicious tacos!

Colombia

Arepas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pre-cooked cornmeal
  • 2 1/2 cups warm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for cooking)
  • Butter (optional, for serving)

Instructions:

1. Make the dough:
In a large bowl, mix the pre-cooked cornmeal and salt. Gradually add the warm water, stirring with your hands or a spoon until the dough is smooth and free of lumps. Let the dough rest for 5 minutes.
2. Shape the arepas:
Divide the dough into 6-8 equal portions and shape each one into a ball. Flatten the balls between your palms to form patties about 1/2 inch thick.
3. Cook the arepas:
Heat a little vegetable oil in a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Cook the arepas for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy on the outside. You can also finish cooking them in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for an additional 10 minutes if desired.
4. Serve:
Slice the arepas open and fill them with your favorite ingredients, such as cheese, avocado, shredded meat, or beans. Serve warm with butter or any fillings of your choice.

Churrasco (Grilled Steak)

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds (900g) skirt steak or flank steak
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

1. Marinate the steak:
In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, minced garlic, lime juice, red wine vinegar, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Place the steak in a large resealable bag or shallow dish and pour the marinade over it. Let the steak marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 4 hours in the refrigerator for deeper flavor.
2. Preheat the grill:
Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Make sure the grates are clean and lightly oiled to prevent sticking.
3. Grill the steak:
Remove the steak from the marinade and let any excess drip off. Grill the steak for about 3-5 minutes per side for medium-rare, or adjust the cooking time to your preferred doneness. Use tongs to flip the steak once during cooking.
4. Rest and serve:
Remove the steak from the grill and let it rest for 5-10 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute. Slice the steak against the grain into thin strips. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley if desired, and serve with chimichurri sauce, roasted vegetables, or a fresh salad.

Brazil

Empanadas

Ingredients:

For the dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (chilled and cubed)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg
  • 3/4 cup cold water (add more if needed)

For the filling:

  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped olives (optional)
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
  • 1 hard-boiled egg, chopped (optional)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

1. Make the dough:
In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
In a separate small bowl, beat the egg and mix it with cold water. Gradually add the egg mixture to the flour, stirring until a dough forms. Knead the dough lightly until smooth, then wrap it in plastic wrap and chill for at least 30 minutes.
2. Prepare the filling:
In a skillet over medium heat, sauté the chopped onion and garlic until softened. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up as it cooks. Stir in paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Add olives, raisins, and chopped egg if using, then remove from heat and let cool slightly.
3. Assemble the empanadas:
Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the dough on a floured surface until it's about 1/8 inch thick. Use a round cutter (4-6 inches in diameter) to cut out circles of dough.
Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the dough over to form a half-moon shape and press the edges together to seal. Crimp the edges with a fork for a decorative finish.
4. Bake the empanadas:
Transfer the empanadas to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Beat an egg and brush it over the tops of the empanadas for a golden finish. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
5. Serve:
Let the empanadas cool slightly before serving. Enjoy them with your favorite dipping sauces like chimichurri or salsa.
