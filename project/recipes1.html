pelmeni

Ingredients:

For the dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water (lukewarm)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (optional, for a softer dough)

For the filling:

  • 250g (½ lb) ground beef
  • 250g (½ lb) ground pork (you can also use just beef or a mix of other meats)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped or grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cold water (to keep the filling juicy)

Instructions:

1. Make the dough:
In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. In a separate bowl, beat the egg, and mix it with water and oil. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the flour, mixing to form a dough. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with a clean cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
2. Prepare the filling:
In a large bowl, combine the ground beef and pork. Add the finely chopped onion, garlic, salt, pepper, and cold water. Mix well until everything is evenly combined.
3. Roll and fill the pelmeni:
On a floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/16-inch thickness. Use a round cutter (about 2 to 3 inches in diameter) to cut out circles from the dough. Place a small amount (about 1 teaspoon) of filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape, pinching the edges to seal. Then, bring the two ends together and pinch to form a traditional pelmeni shape (similar to tortellini).
4. Cook the pelmeni:
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pelmeni in batches and cook for about 4-5 minutes, or until they float to the surface. Remove the cooked pelmeni with a slotted spoon.
5. Serve:
Serve hot with butter, sour cream, or a sprinkle of fresh dill. You can also drizzle with vinegar or enjoy them in broth for a soup-like version. Enjoy your delicious homemade Pelmeni!

Khuushuur

Ingredients:

For the dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water (lukewarm)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For the filling:

  • 500g (1 lb) ground beef or lamb (or a mixture of both)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1-2 tablespoons water (to keep the filling moist)
  • Optional: 1-2 teaspoons cumin or coriander for extra flavor

Instructions:

1. Make the dough:
In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add lukewarm water to the flour, mixing it until it forms a dough. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes, until smooth and elastic. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
2. Prepare the filling:
In a large bowl, mix the ground meat, chopped onion, garlic (if using), salt, pepper, and any optional spices. Add a tablespoon or two of water to the filling to keep it juicy and tender.
3. Roll and fill the khuushuur:
Divide the dough into small portions (about the size of a golf ball). Roll each portion into a thin circle, about 4-5 inches in diameter. Place a spoonful of the meat filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough in half to form a half-moon shape and pinch the edges tightly to seal.
4. Cook the khuushuur:
Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the khuushuur in batches until they are golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.
5. Serve:
Serve hot, traditionally with a side of salad or dipping sauce, or enjoy them on their own.

Bibimbap

Ingredients:

For the rice:

  • 2 cups short-grain white rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water

For the toppings:

  • 1 large carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, julienned
  • 200g (7 oz) spinach, blanched and squeezed
  • 100g (3.5 oz) bean sprouts, blanched
  • 200g (7 oz) shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 200g (7 oz) ground beef or bulgogi (optional)
  • 1 egg (per serving)

For the sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions:

1. Prepare the rice:
Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Add rice and water to a rice cooker or pot, and cook until done.
2. Prepare the vegetables:
Blanch the spinach and bean sprouts separately in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then rinse with cold water. Squeeze the excess water out of the spinach and toss with a pinch of salt and sesame oil. Season the bean sprouts with a pinch of salt. Sauté the carrots, zucchini, and mushrooms separately in a little oil until softened, about 2-3 minutes for each.
3. Prepare the beef (optional):
In a pan, cook the ground beef or bulgogi with a little oil, seasoning with salt and pepper (or soy sauce if using bulgogi).
4. Prepare the sauce:
In a small bowl, mix together gochujang, sesame oil, rice vinegar, sugar, sesame seeds, and minced garlic. Adjust spice level to taste by adding more or less gochujang.
5. Assemble the bibimbap:
In a large bowl, place a portion of cooked rice at the bottom. Arrange the cooked vegetables, mushrooms, and beef on top in sections. Fry an egg sunny side up and place it in the center. Drizzle with sesame oil and add a dollop of the sauce on top.
6. Serve:
Mix everything together thoroughly before eating. Enjoy your delicious Bibimbap!

Dim Sum

Ingredients:

For the dough (dumpling wrappers):

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup hot water
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • Pinch of salt

For the filling:

  • 200g (7 oz) ground pork
  • 100g (3.5 oz) shrimp, finely chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine or sherry
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. Prepare the dough:
In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the hot water, mixing with a spoon or chopsticks until the mixture becomes crumbly. Add the cold water and knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let the dough rest for 30 minutes.
2. Prepare the filling:
In a bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped shrimp, onion, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, rice wine, ginger, and seasoning. Mix well until evenly combined.
3. Roll out the wrappers:
Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a walnut). Roll each ball into a thin circle, about 3 inches in diameter. Keep the dough circles covered with a damp cloth to prevent them from drying out.
4. Fill the dumplings:
Place a small amount of filling (about 1 teaspoon) in the center of each wrapper. Fold the wrapper over the filling to create a half-moon shape, then pleat and pinch the edges to seal.
5. Steam the dim sum:
Place the dumplings in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper or cabbage leaves. Steam over boiling water for about 8-10 minutes, or until the filling is cooked through.
6. Serve:
Serve hot with soy sauce, chili oil, or your favorite dipping sauce. Enjoy your homemade Dim Sum!

Sushi

Ingredients:

For the sushi rice:

  • 2 cups sushi rice (short-grain rice)
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the sushi rolls (choose your fillings):

  • Nori (seaweed sheets)
  • Fresh raw fish (salmon, tuna) or cooked shrimp
  • Cucumber, julienned
  • Avocado, sliced
  • Carrots, julienned
  • Pickled ginger
  • Wasabi (optional)
  • Soy sauce (for dipping)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the sushi rice:
Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Add the rice and water to a rice cooker or pot, and cook until the rice is done.
While the rice is cooking, combine rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small saucepan. Heat until the sugar dissolves, then let it cool.
When the rice is done, transfer it to a large bowl. Gradually pour the vinegar mixture over the rice while gently folding the rice with a wooden spoon. Let the rice cool to room temperature.
2. Prepare the sushi fillings:
Slice the fish, cucumber, avocado, and carrots into thin strips.
3. Assemble the sushi rolls:
Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat (shiny side down). Spread a thin, even layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving a small border at the top. Add your choice of fillings (fish, cucumber, avocado, etc.) in a line across the middle of the rice.
Roll the sushi tightly using the bamboo mat, pressing firmly to keep the roll intact. Seal the edge with a little water.
4. Slice the sushi rolls:
Using a sharp knife, slice the sushi roll into bite-sized pieces. Clean the knife between cuts to keep the slices clean and smooth.
5. Serve:
Arrange the sushi rolls on a plate and serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger. Enjoy your fresh sushi!
