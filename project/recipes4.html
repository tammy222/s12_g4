Paella

Ingredients:

For the paella:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads (soaked in 2 tablespoons warm water)
  • 1 1/2 cups Bomba or Arborio rice (short-grain rice)
  • 4 cups chicken or seafood stock
  • 1/2 cup peas (optional)
  • 500g (1 lb) seafood mix (shrimp, mussels, squid)
  • 250g (½ lb) chicken thighs or rabbit, cut into pieces (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Sauté the ingredients:
In a large paella pan or wide skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chicken (if using) and brown on all sides, then set aside. In the same pan, sauté the onion, garlic, and red bell pepper until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the grated tomatoes and smoked paprika, cooking for another 2 minutes.
2. Add the rice and stock:
Stir in the rice and saffron-infused water, ensuring the rice is well-coated in the tomato mixture. Pour in the chicken or seafood stock, and bring the mixture to a simmer. Add salt and pepper to taste. Lower the heat and let the rice cook, uncovered, for about 15 minutes without stirring.
3. Add the seafood:
Arrange the seafood (shrimp, mussels, squid) on top of the rice, along with the browned chicken (if using). Add the peas if desired. Continue to cook for another 10-15 minutes, or until the seafood is fully cooked and the rice has absorbed the liquid.
4. Rest and serve:
Once the paella is done, remove it from the heat and cover with a clean towel. Let it rest for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges. Enjoy your delicious Spanish Paella!

Couscous

Ingredients:

For the couscous:

  • 1 1/2 cups couscous
  • 1 1/2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

Optional add-ins:

  • 1/4 cup raisins or dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup slivered almonds, toasted
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the couscous:
In a medium saucepan, bring the water or vegetable broth to a boil. Stir in the olive oil, salt, and pepper. Remove from heat and stir in the couscous. Cover and let it sit for 5 minutes, or until the couscous has absorbed the liquid.
2. Fluff the couscous:
After 5 minutes, uncover the saucepan and use a fork to fluff the couscous, separating the grains. Add any optional add-ins like raisins, almonds, or herbs at this point and stir gently to combine.
3. Serve:
Transfer the couscous to a serving dish and garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro. Serve with lemon wedges on the side for added brightness. Enjoy your light and fluffy couscous!

Steamed Mussels

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds (1 kg) fresh mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon butter (optional)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. Sauté the aromatics:
In a large pot or deep skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened and fragrant, about 3 minutes. If using, stir in the red pepper flakes for heat and cook for another 30 seconds.
2. Add the broth and mussels:
Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth and diced tomatoes (if using), and bring the mixture to a simmer. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Add the cleaned mussels to the pot, stirring to coat them in the liquid.
3. Steam the mussels:
Cover the pot and steam the mussels for 5-7 minutes, or until they open up. Shake the pot occasionally to ensure even cooking. Discard any mussels that do not open.
4. Finish and serve:
Remove from heat and stir in the butter (if using) for added richness. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges. Enjoy your steamed mussels with crusty bread for dipping in the flavorful broth!

Saskatoon Pie

Ingredients:

For the pie crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar

For the filling:

  • 4 cups Saskatoon berries (fresh or frozen)
  • 3/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (for thickening)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon butter (cut into small pieces, for dotting the pie)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the pie crust:
In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the chilled butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough starts to come together. Divide the dough in half, shape into discs, wrap in plastic wrap, and chill for at least 30 minutes.
2. Make the filling:
In a large bowl, combine the Saskatoon berries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, cinnamon, and nutmeg (if using). Stir gently to coat the berries and set aside while you roll out the dough.
3. Assemble the pie:
Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll out one disc of dough on a floured surface and line a 9-inch pie pan with it. Pour the berry filling into the pie crust, spreading it evenly. Dot the top with small pieces of butter.
Roll out the second disc of dough and place it over the filling. Trim and crimp the edges to seal. Cut a few small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
4. Bake the pie:
Bake the pie for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. If the edges start to brown too quickly, cover them with foil.
5. Cool and serve:
Let the pie cool for at least 2 hours before slicing to allow the filling to set. Serve with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream. Enjoy your delicious Saskatoon Pie!

Burger

Ingredients:

For the burger patties:

  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef (80% lean for juicier burgers)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce (optional)

For assembling the burger:

  • 4 burger buns
  • Cheese slices (cheddar, American, or your choice)
  • 4 lettuce leaves
  • 4 tomato slices
  • 4 onion rings (optional)
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise (or your preferred condiments)

Instructions:

1. Make the patties:
In a large bowl, mix the ground beef with salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and Worcestershire sauce (if using). Divide the mixture into 4 equal portions and shape them into patties, about 3/4 inch thick. Use your thumb to make a small indentation in the center of each patty to help it cook evenly.
2. Cook the patties:
Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the patties for about 4-5 minutes per side, depending on desired doneness. For cheeseburgers, place a slice of cheese on each patty during the last minute of cooking, covering the pan or grill to help the cheese melt.
3. Toast the buns:
While the patties are cooking, lightly toast the burger buns on the grill or in a toaster.
4. Assemble the burgers:
Spread condiments (ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise) on the bottom half of each bun. Add lettuce, tomato, and onion slices. Place the cooked burger patty on top and add pickles if desired. Cover with the top half of the bun.
5. Serve:
Serve your delicious homemade burgers with your favorite sides like fries, coleslaw, or a fresh salad. Enjoy!
